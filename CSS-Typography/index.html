Small Workers, Big Impact: The Power of Honeybees

The humble honeybee is a powerhouse of global agriculture, responsible for pollinating one-third of the food we eat. As they move from flower to flower searching for nectar, they transfer pollen that allows plants, fruits, and vegetables to reproduce. Beyond securing our food supply, bees support entire ecosystems by helping wild plants grow, which in turn provides habitats for countless other insects and animals. Protecting these tiny, hardworking insects from habitat loss and pesticides is directly tied to safeguarding our own environmental and economic survival.
